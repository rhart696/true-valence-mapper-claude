## SECTION 3: Facilitation Framework (15 min)

### Slide 9: The 6-Phase Coaching Structure

**Visual: Linear flow diagram**

```
Setup â†’ Mapping â†’ First Reaction â†’ Deep Dive â†’ Action Planning â†’ Close
```

**Phase 1: Setup (2-3 min)**
- Frame the exercise
- Ensure privacy and safety
- Set expectations

**Phase 2: Mapping (5-10 min)**
- Let them work in silence
- Observe non-verbals
- Don't interrupt

**Phase 3: First Reaction (3-5 min)**
- "What do you notice?"
- Follow their energy
- Stay open-ended

**Phase 4: Deep Dive (15-20 min)**
- Explore strengths, asymmetries, gaps
- Use structured inquiry
- Connect to stories

**Phase 5: Action Planning (10-15 min)**
- Prioritize 1-3 relationships
- Identify small, concrete actions
- Co-create, don't prescribe

**Phase 6: Close (3-5 min)**
- Synthesize insights
- Confirm commitments
- Schedule follow-up

---

### Slide 10: The Essential 5 Prompts (Memorize These)

**Print these on facilitator cards:**

1. **Opening:** "What do you notice?"  
   *Use: First reaction phase*

2. **Strengths:** "What makes these high-trust relationships work?"  
   *Use: Deep dive - strengths exploration*

3. **Asymmetry:** "What might explain these trust differences?"  
   *Use: Deep dive - pattern analysis*

4. **Priority:** "Which 1-3 relationships matter most right now?"  
   *Use: Action planning - narrowing focus*

5. **Action:** "What's one small step you could take this week?"  
   *Use: Action planning - behavior change*

**Why These Work:**
- Open-ended (not yes/no)
- Client-centered (not advice-giving)
- Forward-moving (not dwelling)

---

### Slide 11: What to Listen For

**Client Language Patterns:**

| What They Say | What It Might Mean | Your Response |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| "They're all fine" | Avoidance, fear of vulnerability | "Let's go deeper. Where could a relationship be stronger?" |
| "I already knew this" | Intellectualizing to avoid feeling | "What are you *feeling* as you look at this?" |
| "This is so depressing" | Overwhelm, may need to slow down | "That's heavy. Let's find one small area of hope." |
| "It's not my fault" | Defensiveness, may feel blamed | "I'm not blaming you. Tell me about your experience." |
| "I can't change anything" | Learned helplessness | "What's one tiny thing that's in your control?" |

**Body Language Cues:**
- Sighing while scoring = emotional weight
- Long pauses = complexity or ambivalence
- Rapid scoring = avoidance or overwhelm
- Tears = vulnerability (good! but slow down)

---

### Slide 12: Dos and Don'ts

**âœ… DO:**
- Stay curious, not prescriptive
- Validate all feelings ("That makes sense")
- Explore their agency and control
- Connect patterns to specific stories
- Hold space for silence (count to 10!)
- Name elephants in the room gently

**âŒ DON'T:**
- Jump to solutions prematurely
- Judge their scores or relationships
- Share results without consent
- Push when they resist
- Assume "low trust = bad person"
- Rush the reflection process

**Golden Rule:** The insights must come from THEM, not you.

## SECTION 5: Advanced Techniques (20 min)

### Slide 16: Dealing with Resistance

**Common Resistance Patterns:**

1. **"I don't want to rate people"**
   - Response: "I get itâ€”ratings feel reductive. These aren't facts, they're your perceptions right now. They can change."
   - Alternative: "Want to start with just 3-4 people and see how it feels?"

2. **"The trust depends on the situation"**
   - Response: "Absolutely. For this exercise, focus on work challenges specifically. What's your overall comfort level?"

3. **"This person will never change"**
   - Response: "Maybe. But you can change how you engage with them. What's in your control?"

4. **"All my relationships are fine"**
   - Response: "Great! Are there any you'd like to strengthen, even if they're already okay?"

---

### Slide 17: Managing Strong Emotion

**If Client Becomes Tearful:**
- Slow down immediately
- Don't rush past the emotion
- "This is bringing up something important. Want to tell me what you're feeling?"
- Validate: "It makes sense this matters to you."
- Don't solveâ€”sit with the feeling

**If Client Expresses Anger:**
- "That sounds really frustrating."
- "Help me understand what's most upsetting."
- Later: "What would help channel that frustration into action?"

**If Client Shuts Down:**
- "I notice you've gotten quiet. What's happening?"
- "We can pause anytime. Do you want to take a break?"
- "What would make this feel safer?"

**Red Flag:** If emotion is disproportionate or persistent, consider:
- Unresolved trauma
- Mental health concerns
- Need for specialist referral

---

### Slide 18: Power Dynamics & Hierarchy

**When Clients Map Their Boss:**

**Watch For:**
- Fear of judgment: "My boss should trust me moreâ€”what's wrong with me?"
- Learned helplessness: "There's nothing I can do about this"
- Boundary confusion: "They overshare and I don't know how to handle it"

**Helpful Reframes:**
- "Hierarchical relationships have different dynamics. That's not your fault."
- "You can influence this relationship even if you're not in charge of it."
- "Trust with authority figures often develops differently than peer trust."

**Coaching Moves:**
- Explore power dynamics explicitly
- Distinguish "influence" from "control"
- Discuss upward relationship management
- Consider if the boss is the problem (toxic leadership)

### Slide 20: Ethical Boundaries

**You Are a Coach, Not a Therapist:**
- Coaches facilitate growth and performance
- Therapists treat mental health conditions
- Know the difference; honor the boundary

**Client Owns the Data:**
You cannot:
- Share results without explicit consent
- Use for performance reviews or HR reports
- Discuss with managers or colleagues
- Keep copies without permission

**Exception:** Mandatory reporting (harm to self/others, child abuse)
- If you must report, tell the client first
- Explain why and what will happen
- Maintain compassion

**When in Doubt:** Consult with your supervisor or ProActive leadership.

---

### Slide 21: Facilitator Self-Care

**This Work Is Emotionally Demanding:**

You're holding space for:
- Vulnerability and pain
- Disappointment and frustration
- Sometimes trauma and grief

**Protect Yourself:**
- Debrief difficult sessions with a colleague
- Don't carry their problems home
- Recognize when you're triggered
- Take breaks between sessions
- Celebrate wins (however small)

**Remember:**
- You're a guide, not a savior
- Their growth is their responsibility
- You can't want it more than they do
- It's okay to feel stuck sometimes

**Resources:**

## SECTION 6: Q&A & Troubleshooting (10 min)

### Slide 22: Common Questions

**Q: "How long should a session using this tool take?"**
A: 45-60 minutes typically. 10 min mapping, 30 min exploration, 10 min action planning.

**Q: "Can I use this in team coaching?"**
A: Yes, with caution. Individual mapping is private. Anonymous aggregates can inform team discussions about patterns.

**Q: "What if a client wants to add 15 people?"**
A: Redirect to quality over quantity. "Let's focus on the 6-8 who most affect your daily experience."

**Q: "What if they score everyone as 0 or 3 (extremes only)?"**
A: Explore all-or-nothing thinking. "Is nuance hard to capture, or do relationships really feel black-and-white?"

**Q: "Can I use this for remote/async coaching?"**
A: Absolutely. Send link 24-48 hours before session. Review together live.

**Q: "How often should clients revisit their map?"**
A: Every 3-6 months for progress tracking. More frequently during active relationship work.
