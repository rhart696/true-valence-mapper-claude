### SESSION STRUCTURE (45-60 minutes)

| Phase | Time | Key Actions |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **1. Setup** | 2-3 min | Frame the exercise, ensure privacy, set expectations |
| **2. Mapping** | 5-10 min | Silent observation, note non-verbals, let them drive |
| **3. First Reaction** | 3-5 min | "What do you notice?" Open-ended only. |
| **4. Deep Dive** | 15-20 min | Explore patterns: strengths ‚Üí asymmetries ‚Üí gaps |
| **5. Action Plan** | 10-15 min | Prioritize 1-3 relationships, identify small steps |
| **6. Close** | 3-5 min | Synthesize insights, schedule follow-up |

---

### ESSENTIAL PROMPTS (Memorize These 5)

1. **Opening:** "What do you notice?"
2. **Strengths:** "What makes these high-trust relationships work?"
3. **Asymmetry:** "What might explain these trust differences?"
4. **Priority:** "Which 1-3 relationships matter most right now?"
5. **Action:** "What's one small step you could take this week?"

### RED FLAGS ‚Üí ESCALATE

**When to pause or refer out:**
- All relationships scored 0-1 (possible depression, trauma, neurodivergence)
- Client expresses suicidal ideation or severe hopelessness
- Evidence of workplace abuse or harassment
- Client unable to engage reflectively (acute crisis state)

**Response:** "This seems like it's touching something deeper. Would it help to talk with [mental health resource]?"

---

### FACILITATION REMINDERS

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Stay curious, not prescriptive
- Validate all feelings
- Explore client's agency
- Connect patterns to concrete stories
- Name elephants in room gently
- Hold space for silence

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Jump to solutions
- Judge their scores or relationships
- Share tool results with HR/management without consent
- Push when they resist
- Assume "low trust = bad person"
- Rush the reflection

### COMMON STUCK POINTS

**"I don't know how to rate them"**
‚Üí "Use your gut. If you had to pick right now, what feels true?"

**"The trust depends on the situation"**
‚Üí "For work challenges specifically, what's your overall comfort level?"

**"Can I add more than 8 people?"**
‚Üí "Let's focus on quality over quantity. Who most affects your daily experience?"

**"This person will never change"**
‚Üí "Maybe. But you can change how you engage with them. What's in your control?"

2. One small action ("This week I'll...")
3. One accountability touchpoint ("We'll check in on [date]")

## üìä PATTERN RECOGNITION CHEAT SHEET

### High Trust Everywhere (All 2-3)
**Interpret:** Strong network, possible conflict avoidance, or easy-going environment  
**Explore:** "Is there anyone you're holding back from being fully honest with?"

### Low Trust Everywhere (All 0-1)
**Interpret:** Possible burnout, toxic environment, or trust issues  
**Explore:** "What's making trust so hard right now?"

### You Trust High / They Trust Low (Asymmetry)
**Interpret:** You're over-giving, they're holding back, or power dynamics  
**Explore:** "What stops them from coming to you?"

### They Trust High / You Trust Low (Asymmetry)
**Interpret:** You're guarded, they're vulnerable, or you're unsure  
**Explore:** "What would it take for you to feel safer with them?"

### One Outlier (One red, rest green)
**Interpret:** Specific relationship issue, past conflict, or mismatch  
**Explore:** "Tell me about this relationship. What's the story here?"

## üí° ADVANCED MOVES

### The Reframe
**Client:** "I can't trust anyone here."  
**You:** "What if trust isn't binary? Where's there even 10% trust?"

### The Exception Question
**Client:** "All my relationships are bad."  
**You:** "When's a time one of these relationships worked, even briefly?"

### The Future Self
**Client:** "I don't know what to do."  
**You:** "Imagine it's 6 months from now and this has improved. What did you do?"

### The Scaling Question
**Client:** "The relationship with Sarah is terrible."  
**You:** "On a scale of 1-10, where is it now? What would move it up even one point?"
