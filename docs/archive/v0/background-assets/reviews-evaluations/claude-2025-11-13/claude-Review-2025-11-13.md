### **The Trust Assessment Process**

**Cognitive Load Concerns:**
- Asking users to simultaneously consider 8 people AND assess bidirectional trust (16 data points minimum) could be overwhelming
- The 1-3 scale is appropriately simple, but what do these numbers mean emotionally? 
  - Is "1" = "zero confidence" or "some confidence"? 
  - Is "3" = "complete confidence" or "high confidence"?

**Missing Nuance:**
- Real workplace relationships are messy. Where's the option for:
  - "I don't know" (crucial for new relationships)
  - "It depends on the situation" (trust is often domain-specific)
  - Asymmetric power dynamics (boss vs. peer relationships function differently)

**Recommendation:** 
- Consider a 0-3 scale (0 = no trust established, 1 = low, 2 = moderate, 3 = high)
- Add optional context tags: "New Relationship," "In Transition," "Power Dynamic"
- Allow users to mark relationships as "Skip for now"

---

### **The Questions Themselves**

**Current wording:** 
- Outward: "How confident am I that I'd go to them with a problem?"
- Inward: "How confident am I that they'd come to me with a problem?"

**Issues:**
1. **"A problem" is vague** - Work problem? Personal problem? Conflict with them? This ambiguity could lead to inconsistent self-assessment
2. **The inward question asks users to mind-read** - "How confident am I that they'd come to me" requires speculation about others' perceptions
3. **Problem-focused framing is limiting** - Trust extends beyond problem-solving (collaboration, celebration, vulnerability, advocacy)

**Better Framing:**
- Outward: "How comfortable would I feel bringing a **work challenge** to this person for support or guidance?"
- Inward: "How comfortable do **I think** this person would feel bringing a **work challenge** to me?"

Or even better, offer **multiple trust dimensions:**
- Competence trust: "I trust their judgment"
- Character trust: "I trust their intentions"
- Reliability trust: "I trust they'll follow through"
- Vulnerability trust: "I'd share concerns with them"
